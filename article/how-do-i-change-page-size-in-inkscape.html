How Do I Change Page Size in Inkscape?

This article provides a quick and straightforward guide on how to modify document properties in Inkscape, focusing specifically on adjusting your page size. Whether you need to switch to a standard print format or set up a custom canvas for a unique digital illustration, you will learn the exact steps, menus, and shortcuts needed to perfectly configure your workspace.

Accessing Document Properties

The first step to changing your page size is opening the Document Properties dialog box. You can access this in two intuitive ways:

  • Using the Menu: Navigate to the top menu bar, click on File, and select Document Properties from the dropdown list.
  • Using a Keyboard Shortcut: Press Shift + Ctrl + D (Windows/Linux) or Shift + Cmd + D (Mac) to open the menu instantly.

Modifying the Page Size

Once the Document Properties window is open, ensure you are on the Display or Page tab (the naming varies slightly depending on your specific version of Inkscape). From here, you have a few distinct ways to adjust your canvas:

  • Pre-defined Formats: Look for the “Format” section. Inkscape provides a comprehensive list of standard sizes, such as A4, US Letter, business cards, and various standard screen resolutions. Simply clicking on one of these options will automatically update your canvas in the background.
  • Custom Dimensions: If you need a highly specific size for a unique project, locate the “Custom size” section. Here, you can manually type your desired numerical values directly into the Width and Height fields.
  • Changing Units: Before typing in custom dimensions, it is best practice to use the “Units” dropdown menu located near the width and height fields. This allows you to select your preferred measurement system, such as millimeters (mm), inches (in), or pixels (px).

Resizing the Page to Your Artwork

Inkscape also offers a highly useful feature to automatically snap the page dimensions to the artwork you have already created on the canvas. Inside the Document Properties menu, look for an expandable option labeled Resize page to content (or “Resize page to drawing or selection” in older versions). Expanding this and clicking the resize button will instantly shrink or expand your document borders to perfectly frame your existing vector graphics, eliminating any unnecessary blank space.
